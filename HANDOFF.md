# Campus Closet Website -- E-Board Guide

Welcome! This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about managing the Campus Closet website. No technical experience is needed -- just follow the step-by-step instructions below.

---

## 1. Welcome and Overview

The Campus Closet website has two parts:

- **The public site** -- This is what everyone sees when they visit your website. It includes information about Campus Closet, upcoming events, the donation page with a map, the FAQ, your team bios, and a photo gallery. No login is needed to view any of this.
- **The admin portal** -- This is a private area where you (the e-board) can manage all the content on the public site. You need to log in with your BU Google account to access it.

**Your website address:** https://h4-i-campus-closet.vercel.app

**Who built it:** This website was built by Hack4Impact BU, a student organization that builds software for nonprofits and community organizations.

---

## 2. Accessing the Admin Portal

### How to log in

1. Go to **https://h4-i-campus-closet.vercel.app/admin**
2. Click **Sign in with Google**
3. Sign in with your BU Google account (the same one you use for BU email)

**Important:** Your Google email must be added to the approved admin list before you can log in. If you get an error, see Section 11 (Adding/Removing Admin Users) below.

### What you will see after logging in

Once you log in, you will see the admin portal. On the left side of the screen, there is a navigation menu (the sidebar) with links to every section you can manage:

- **Dashboard** -- Your home base
- **Events** -- Create and manage swap events, drives, and meetings
- **Impact** -- Log environmental impact data after events
- **Donation Bins** -- Manage campus drop-off locations on the map
- **Contact Inbox** -- View messages from visitors
- **Team** -- Manage e-board member bios and photos
- **FAQ** -- Add and edit frequently asked questions
- **Photos** -- Upload event photos for the gallery
- **Site Content** -- Edit text across the entire website

At the bottom of the sidebar, you will also find:
- **View Site** -- Opens the public website so you can see your changes
- **Sign Out** -- Logs you out of the admin portal

## 3. Managing Content (Site Content Page)

The Site Content page lets you edit almost every piece of text on the public website -- without needing to touch any code.

### How to edit text on the site

1. In the sidebar, click **Site Content**
2. Scroll down to find the section you want to edit
3. Change the text in the text box
4. Click the **Save** button next to the field you changed
5. Wait about 60 seconds, then visit the public site to see your changes

The Save button will only become clickable after you have made a change. A green confirmation message will appear when your change is saved.

## 4. Managing Events

### How to create a new event

1. In the sidebar, click **Events**
2. Under "Add Event," fill in the form:
   - **Title** -- The name of your event (e.g., "Spring Clothing Swap")
   - **Type** -- Choose one:
     - **Swap** -- Students browse and take free clothing
     - **Drive** -- Students donate clothing at bins or collection points
     - **Meeting** -- Internal team meetings
   - **Date & Time** -- When the event takes place
   - **Location** -- Where on campus (e.g., "GSU Ballroom")
   - **Description** -- A short description of what attendees can expect
   - **Item Limit** (optional) -- Maximum number of items per person (leave blank if no limit)
3. Click **Add Event**

### How to edit an event

1. Find the event in the list below the form
2. Click **Edit** on the right side
3. Make your changes in the popup window
4. Click **Save**

### How to delete an event

1. Find the event in the list
2. Click **Delete** on the right side
3. Confirm by clicking OK in the popup

### Filtering events

Use the dropdown menu above the event list to filter by type (All, Swaps, Drives, or Meetings).

### Good to know

- Events automatically appear on the public Events page
- Past events are automatically marked as "Past" based on the date -- you do not need to do anything
- Each event type is color-coded: green for Swaps, blue for Drives, and purple for Meetings

## 5. Managing the Team (E-Board)

### How to add a team member

1. In the sidebar, click **Team**
2. Under "Add Member," fill in:
   - **Name** -- Full name
   - **Role** -- Their position (e.g., "President," "VP of Marketing")
   - **Photo** -- Click the upload area or drag and drop a photo (JPG or PNG, max 5 MB)
   - **Bio** -- A short description about the person
3. Click **Add Member**

### How to edit a team member

1. Find the person in the "Current Team" list
2. Click **Edit**
3. Make changes in the popup window (you can update their photo, name, role, or bio)
4. Click **Save**

### How to reorder team members

Team members appear on the public About page in the order shown in the admin portal. To change the order:

1. Find the person you want to move
2. Click the **up arrow** or **down arrow** on the left side of their card

### How to remove a team member

1. Find the person in the list
2. Click **Delete**
3. Confirm by clicking OK

## 7. Managing Photos (Gallery)

### How to upload a photo

1. In the sidebar, click **Photos**
2. Under "Add Photo," click the upload area (or drag and drop an image file)
   - Accepted formats: JPG, PNG, WebP, GIF
   - Maximum file size: 5 MB
3. Optionally add a **Caption** (a short description of the photo)
4. Optionally **tag the photo with an event** by selecting from the dropdown
5. Click **Add Photo**

### How to edit a photo

1. Find the photo in the gallery grid
2. Click **Edit** below the photo
3. Update the caption or event tag
4. Click **Save**

### How to delete a photo

1. Find the photo in the gallery grid
2. Click **Delete** below the photo
3. Confirm by clicking OK

### Good to know

- Photos appear on the public site in the gallery section
- The homepage automatically shows the most recent photos
- Tagging photos with events helps organize them

## 8. Impact Statistics

After each event, you can log environmental impact data that appears on the About page.

### How to add impact data

1. In the sidebar, click **Impact**
2. Under "Add Impact Entry," fill in:
   - **Link to Event** (optional) -- Select the event this data is for, or choose "Global" for overall stats
   - **Items Reused** -- Number of clothing items that were swapped/taken
   - **Items Donated** -- Number of items donated
   - **Attendance** -- Number of people who attended
   - **Waste Diverted (kg)** -- Kilograms of clothing kept out of landfills
   - **Water Saved (L)** -- Liters of water saved by reusing clothing
   - **Carbon Saved (kg)** -- Kilograms of carbon emissions avoided
3. Click **Add Entry**

### How to edit or delete impact data

1. Find the record in the list
2. Click **Edit** to update the numbers, or **Delete** to remove it
3. If editing, make your changes and click **Save**

### Good to know

- These numbers feed into the impact statistics displayed on the About page
- You can link impact data to a specific event or leave it as "Global" for overall totals
- Tip: You do not need to calculate the environmental numbers yourself. A rough estimate is fine -- even small numbers add up over time!

## 9. Donation Bins

### How to add a new bin location

1. In the sidebar, click **Donation Bins**
2. Click the **+ Add Bin** button in the top right
3. In the popup window:
   - **Bin Name** -- A friendly name (e.g., "Warren Towers Bin")
   - **Building** -- The building where it is located (e.g., "Warren Towers")
   - **Click on the map** to place a pin at the exact location
   - **Active checkbox** -- Check this to make the bin visible on the public Donate page map. Uncheck to hide it (e.g., during summer)
4. Click **Add Bin**

### How to edit a bin

1. Find the bin in the table
2. Click **Edit**
3. Make your changes (you can move the pin by clicking a new spot on the map)
4. Click **Save Changes**

### How to delete a bin

1. Find the bin in the table
2. Click **Delete**
3. Confirm in the popup by clicking **Delete**

### How to temporarily hide a bin

If a bin is temporarily unavailable (e.g., building closed for the summer):

1. Click **Edit** on the bin
2. Uncheck the **Active** checkbox
3. Click **Save Changes**

The bin will stay in your records but will not appear on the public map until you reactivate it.

## 10. Contact Inbox

When visitors fill out the contact form on your website, their messages appear here.

### Viewing messages

1. In the sidebar, click **Contact Inbox**
2. You will see a table with all messages, showing:
   - Name
   - Email
   - Type (General, Pickup, or Dropoff request)
   - Status
   - Date received
3. Click **View** on any message to read the full details

### Sorting messages

Click on any column header (Name, Email, Type, Status, or Date) to sort the table. Click the same header again to reverse the sort order.

### Updating message status

Each message has a status that helps you track what has been handled:

1. Click **View** on a message
2. At the bottom of the popup, you will see three status buttons:
   - **New** -- Just received, not yet addressed
   - **Responded** -- You have replied to the person
   - **Completed** -- Fully resolved
3. Click the status you want to set

### Important note

The admin portal does **not** send email replies for you. When someone contacts you through the form, you will need to reply to them separately using your own email (e.g., from campuscloset@bu.edu). After you reply, come back here and mark the message as "Responded."

## 11. Adding and Removing Admin Users

To log in to the admin portal, a person's Google email must be on the approved list. Right now, managing this list requires access to your data dashboard (Supabase). Here is how to do it:

### How to add a new admin

1. Go to **https://supabase.com** and sign in with the Campus Closet account
2. Select your project
3. In the left sidebar, click **Table Editor**
4. Click on the table called **AdminUser**
5. Click **Insert** (or **Insert Row**) at the top
6. In the **email** field, type the person's full Google email (e.g., newmember@bu.edu)
7. Click **Save**

That person can now log in to the admin portal.

### How to remove an admin

1. Follow steps 1-4 above to get to the AdminUser table
2. Find the row with the email you want to remove
3. Click on that row and delete it

### Looking ahead

A built-in admin management page (so you can add/remove admins right from the admin portal) is planned for a future update. In the meantime, follow the steps above or ask the dev team for help.

## 12. Platform Accounts and Access

Your website runs on three online services. Here is what each one does and how to access it.

### Vercel (Website Hosting)

**What it does:** Vercel is the service that makes your website available on the internet. Think of it like the landlord for your website -- it keeps the lights on.

- **Dashboard:** https://vercel.com
- **What you need to know:** The website updates automatically whenever new code changes are made. You should not need to touch Vercel for day-to-day content management.
- **When you might need it:** If the website goes down, you can check the Vercel dashboard for error messages. Look for red indicators or the word "Error" on the deployments page.

### GitHub (Code Storage)

**What it does:** GitHub is where all the code for the website is stored. Think of it like a Google Drive, but specifically for code.

- **Repository:** https://github.com/UgaTheDev/H4I_CampusCloset
- **What you need to know:** You do not need to use GitHub for any day-to-day management. All content changes are made through the admin portal.
- **When you might need it:** Only if you are working with a developer to make changes to the website itself (not the content).

### Supabase (Data Dashboard)

**What it does:** Supabase stores all of your website's information -- events, team members, photos, FAQs, contact messages, and more. It also handles the Google login for the admin portal and stores your uploaded photos.

- **Dashboard:** https://supabase.com
- **When you might need it:**
  - **Adding or removing admin users** (see Section 11 above)
  - **Setting up Google login** (one-time setup, if not already done):
    1. Sign in to Supabase
    2. Select your project
    3. In the left sidebar, click **Authentication**
    4. Click **Providers**
    5. Find **Google** in the list and click on it
    6. Follow the instructions to connect your Google account
  - **Emergency data access:** If something goes wrong and you need to view or edit data directly, go to **Table Editor** in the left sidebar. But for normal use, always use the admin portal instead.

## 13. Troubleshooting

### "I can't log in to the admin portal"

- Make sure you are signing in with your BU Google account
- Your email must be on the approved admin list. Ask a current admin to add you (see Section 11)
- Try clearing your browser cache or using a private/incognito window

### "I made changes but they don't appear on the site"

- Wait about 60 seconds -- changes take a moment to appear
- Do a hard refresh of the page: press **Ctrl + Shift + R** (Windows) or **Cmd + Shift + R** (Mac)
- Try opening the site in a private/incognito window to bypass your browser's cache

### "My photo upload failed"

- Check the file size -- it must be under 5 MB
- Make sure the file is a JPG, PNG, WebP, or GIF
- Try a different photo to rule out a corrupted file
- If the problem continues, try resizing the image to a smaller size before uploading

### "The map on the Donate page doesn't show up"

- The map service (Stadia Maps) may need its access key renewed. Contact the dev team for help.

### "The website is completely down"

1. Go to https://vercel.com and sign in
2. Look at the most recent deployment -- if it shows a red error, there may be a code issue
3. Contact the dev team for help

### "I accidentally deleted something"

- Unfortunately, deletions cannot be undone through the admin portal
- If the data is critical, contact the dev team -- they may be able to recover it from a backup
